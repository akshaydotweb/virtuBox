## ðŸ“ TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM

### Brand Typography (Marketing/Communications)

#### Primary Typeface: Roboto Serif Regular
- **Purpose:** Headlines and premium brand experiences
- **Characteristics:** 
  - Modern serif with minimal design
  - Comfortable next to sans-serif fonts
  - No reader fatigue
  - Decisive curves and terminals
  - Cross between serif and slab typeface

#### Secondary Typeface: Proxima Nova (Regular + Bold)
- **Purpose:** Body copy and supporting content
- **Regular:** Standard body copy
- **Bold:** Labels, links, buttons, emphasis

### Type Hierarchy & Scales

#### Type Setting Rules:

| Context | Case | Rule |
|---------|------|------|
| Headlines | Sentence case | 1 headline per section, no period |
| Titles | Title case | Project/cover names |
| Labels | Title case | Bold, scannable, small sizes |
| Buttons & Links | Sentence case | Consistent international standard |
| Numbers/Stats | Proxima Nova | Larger stats can use Roboto Serif |

#### Type Hierarchy Variations:
- **Print & Digital** - Dedicated type scales
- **Desktop Web** - Optimized for screens
- **Tablet & Mobile** - Responsive scaling

### Email & Product Typography
- **Email:** Georgia (fallback for Roboto Serif), Helvetica (fallback for Proxima Nova)
- **Product:** System fonts (San Francisco, Roboto)

### Key Rules:
âœ… **DO:**
- Use sentence case for headlines
- Use 50-80 characters per line
- Use curly quotes and apostrophes
- Use Space 400 after paragraphs
- Use documented font weights/colors/sizes
- Maintain left-aligned paragraphs
- Avoid orphans and widows

âŒ **DON'T:**
- Use periods at end of headlines
- Use uppercase for lines >3 words
- Use hyphens for line breaks
- Use typewriter apostrophes
- Justify text or right-align
- Use undocumented variations

## âœï¸ WRITING SYSTEM

### Brand Voice & Tone (4 Core Tenets)

#### 1. **UNEXPECTED**
*Taking intentional risks and surprising the audience*

**What it means:**
- Extraordinary and beyond typical
- Takes calculated risks
- Sees social differently
- Steps outside industry norms

**What it doesn't mean:**
- Abrupt or unplanned
- Reckless or accidental
- Violates social norms
- Gimmicks or schemes

**Tips:**
- Be transparent and upfront
- Avoid jargon (use synonyms for typical words)
- Break the fourth wall, connect directly
- Share unconventional thoughts

**Avoid:**
- Making unproven claims
- Directly attacking competitors
- Being trendy or shocking for shock value

**Use in:** Emails, ads, social media, landing pages, speaking engagements, thought leadership

#### 2. **MASTERY**
*Demonstrating deep expertise without being condescending*

**What it means:**
- Deep comprehension of product, industry, customers
- Balance between knowledge and superiority
- Never patronizing or pompous
- Respects user intelligence

**Tips:**
- Know when to be humble
- Assume intelligence, not knowledge
- Explain jargon and acronyms
- Use active voice and active verbs
- Be direct and confident
- Be concise (omit needless words)

**Avoid:**
- Condescending words: "actually," "everyone knows," "just," "never," "always"
- Long/complex sentences
- Multiple words with same meaning
- Unnecessary modifiers
- ClichÃ©s and clunky expressions

**Use in:** Blog articles, case studies, product announcements, guides, press releases, web copy

#### 3. **RELATABLE**
*Speaking directly to audience's experiences and emotions*

**What it means:**
- Authority who understands their level
- "We just get it" feeling
- Personal stories and industry-specific examples
- Using everyday language

**Tips:**
- Be conversational with familiar language
- Evoke emotion (pain points, motivations)
- Ask questions to engage and validate

**Avoid:**
- Too much self-deprecation (undermines mastery)
- Centering yourself or Sprout excessively
- Too juvenile or condescending tone

**Use in:** Social content, employer brand, product education, editorial/thought leadership, speaking engagements

#### 4. **INTENTIONAL**
*Balanced, confident, and purposeful communication*

**What it means:**
- Concise, focused, thoughtful
- Every word matters
- Balances all other tenets
- Knows rules to break them

**Tips:**
- Be honest, say what you mean
- Keep it concise (few words, big feels)
- Be consistent with purpose
- Focus on value, not process

**Avoid:**
- Marketing jargon and clichÃ©s
- Needless words
- Eliminate -ly words (actually, really, simply, etc.)

**Use in:** ALL content - it's the balance to everything
